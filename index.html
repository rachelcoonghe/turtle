Turtle Hatchery in Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka's Turtle Population

Sri Lanka is home to five of the world's seven species of sea turtles, including the Green Turtle, Olive Ridley Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, and Leatherback Turtle. These turtles are an important part of Sri Lanka's marine ecosystem and play a crucial role in maintaining a healthy balance in the ocean. Unfortunately, sea turtle populations in Sri Lanka and around the world have been declining rapidly due to human activities such as poaching, egg harvesting, pollution, and habitat destruction. Sri Lanka has recognized the importance of protecting these magnificent creatures and has taken steps to conserve sea turtle populations through conservation efforts.

FACTS ABOUT TURTLES!

TURTLES DON’T HAVE TEETH!

They use their beak-like mouth to grasp their food. This beak is made of keratin (the same stuff your fingernails are made of).

THEY’RE WELL ‘ARD!

Turtle shells are made of over 50 bones fused together - so they're literally wearing their bones on the outside. They also have light, spongy bones that help them float.

THEY ALWAYS RETURN HOME!

Females return to the same beach they hatched on, to lay their own eggs and bury them in sand ‘nests’.
